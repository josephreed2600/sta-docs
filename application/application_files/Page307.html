FAQ’s

Isn’t there already a Sherman Thomas school?

Sherman Thomas STEM Academy is the newest member of the Sherman Thomas organization.  Our sister schools are Sherman Thomas Charter School (a TK-8 model that opened in 2003) and Sherman Thomas     Charter High School (an independent study hybrid model that opened in 2009).

 

What is a charter school?

Charter schools are public schools regulated by federal and state law.  Charter schools are tuition-free and open to all students, non-sectarian, and do not discriminate on any basis. They are publicly funded by local, state, and federal tax dollars based on enrollment, like other public schools. They are held accountable to state and federal academic standards.  As free public schools of choice, charter schools do not engage in selective admissions policies and must accept all students, including students with disabilities and English Learners (ELs), regardless of previous academic performance. Charter schools must use a process to randomly select students, often times a lottery system, if there are more interested students than available seats.  (Taken from California Charter Schools Association website, www.ccsa.org.)

 

How do I get my child signed up?

Follow the application process outlined on page one of this packet.  If we have more students apply by the end of an enrollment period than we have spots for, we will have a random public lottery early in the following month.

When will I know if my student has been accepted?                                   

The application deadline is 3:00 pm on November 30 (fall enrollment period) and April 30 (spring enrollment period). At that time, if the number of applicants exceeds the number of available openings, we will hold a random public lottery to select applicants to be admitted. The lottery will also determine the sequence of applicants on our waiting list. This lottery will be held within a few weeks of the close of the enrollment period, and the public is welcome (but not required) to attend. Contact Ms. Brock for a specific date for the public lottery.

All applicants selected during the lottery process will be given an enrollment packet to be completed. Students with incomplete packets by the deadline will be removed from the admittance list and will be placed at the bottom of the waiting list. We will fill that opening with the next student on the waiting list, who will then have a limited time to complete  the enrollment packet or move to the end of the waiting list.

Please call our office for deadlines or to check your application status at any time.
